Certainly. The fundamental difference lies in their purpose and outcome regarding genetic variation:
1
Mitosis (Cloning)

Produces two genetically identical diploid somatic cells. There is no crossing over, ensuring genetic consistency for growth and tissue repair.

2
Meiosis (Diversity)

Produces four genetically unique haploid gametes. Genetic variation is introduced primarily through 'crossing over' during Prophase I and independent assortment during Metaphase I.
